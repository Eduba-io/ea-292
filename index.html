01 / The Eduba frame

Most aerospace startups automate the wrong things.

A working view of a two-person aerospace team. About 60 percent of what happens every day is traditional database and documentation work. About 30 percent is rule-based logic. Around 10 percent is a real AI problem. The work is sorting those three before the first contract lands.

For Spargo that means a clear boundary between the propulsion bet and the autonomy bet, a traceability layer that the next hire can read without a meeting, and a first-contract capture cadence that runs without the founder sitting in every call.

The 2000-kg StarVault is a physics problem. The two-to-twenty handoff is an operating-system problem. Different work. Different layers. Eduba sits on the second.

60 / 30 / 10 mapped to Spargo
  1. 60%Docs & data
    Requirements. Test matrices. Vendor data packages. ICDs.
  2. 30%Rule-based
    Gate reviews. Go-no-go. Anomaly triage.
  3. 10%Genuine AI
    GNC autonomy. Relative nav. Closed-loop transfer control.
